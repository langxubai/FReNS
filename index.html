Fundamental Research in Natural Sciences Seminar

Introduction

The Fundamental Research in Natural Sciences Seminar is a weekly lectures dedicated to exploring cutting-edge developments across physics, mathematics, computer science, and interdisciplinary approaches to understanding the natural world. By bringing together leading researchers and graduate students, we aim to:

  • Foster in-depth discussions on foundational questions
  • Showcase emerging techniques and methodologies
  • Stimulate collaborations across traditional disciplinary boundaries

Whether you’re working on quantum information, complex systems, condensed matter physics, or theoretical biology, you will find new perspectives and colleagues here.

Our advantage is that we discuss a topic several times and even organize short courses to ensure that attendees can thoroughly understand the content of the direction.
