Fundamental Research in Natural Sciences Seminar

Introduction

The Fundamental Research in Natural Sciences Seminar is a weekly seminar dedicated to exploring cutting-edge developments across physics, mathematics, computer science, and interdisciplinary approaches to understanding the natural world. By bringing together leading researchers and graduate students, we aim to:

  • Foster in-depth discussions on foundational questions
  • Showcase emerging techniques and methodologies
  • Stimulate collaborations across traditional disciplinary boundaries

Whether you’re working on quantum information, complex systems, condensed matter physics, or theoretical biology, you will find new perspectives and colleagues here.

A unique feature of FReNS is our depth of engagement: we often dedicate multiple sessions to a single topic or organize short courses, ensuring attendees gain a rigorous and thorough understanding.
